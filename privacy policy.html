Privacy Policy of IIHC SCHOOL

Last Updated: [Insert Date, e.g., May 18, 2025]

Integrated Innovation and Hospitality Colleges (IIHC SCHOOL) is committed to protecting the privacy and security of the personal information of our students, parents, faculty, staff, and website visitors. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your information in accordance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173) and its implementing rules and regulations.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect personal information through various means, including:

  • Information you provide directly: When you interact with us through our website (e.g., filling out contact forms, online registration, application forms, login), during enrollment, when participating in school activities, or through other communications. This may include your name, contact details (address, email, phone number), date of birth, academic records, family information, payment information, and other details relevant to your relationship with the school.
  • Information collected automatically: When you visit our website, we may automatically collect certain information about your device and usage, such as your IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URLs, pages viewed, and the dates and times of your visits. This information is typically collected using cookies and similar technologies.
  • Information from other sources: We may receive information about you from third parties with your consent or as otherwise permitted by law, such as from previous schools or educational institutions.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the personal information we collect for legitimate school purposes, including but not limited to:

  • Processing applications for admission and enrollment.
  • Managing student academic records and progress.
  • Communicating with students and parents/guardians regarding academic matters, school events, and important updates.
  • Providing educational services and support.
  • Managing school administration, including billing and payments.
  • Ensuring the safety and security of our campus and community.
  • Responding to inquiries and requests submitted through our website or other channels.
  • Improving our website, programs, and services.
  • Complying with legal and regulatory requirements, including reporting to relevant government agencies (e.g., DepEd, CHED, TESDA).
  • For research and statistical purposes to improve our educational offerings (data used will be aggregated and anonymized where possible).

3. Disclosure of Your Information

We may disclose your personal information to third parties only when necessary for the purposes outlined in this policy, with your consent, or when required by law. This may include:

  • Relevant government agencies (DepEd, CHED, TESDA, etc.) for reporting and compliance purposes.
  • Service providers who perform functions on our behalf (e.g., IT support, payment processing, security services), under strict confidentiality agreements.
  • Other educational institutions or employers, with your consent, for purposes of transcripts or employment verification.
  • In response to legal processes, court orders, or government requests.
  • To protect the rights, property, or safety of IIHC SCHOOL, our community, or others.

We will not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for their marketing purposes without your explicit consent.

4. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical, organizational, and physical security measures to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. However, please be aware that no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure.

5. Cookies and Other Technologies

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to enhance your Browse experience, analyze website traffic, and for other operational purposes. You can control the use of cookies through your browser settings.

6. Links to Third-Party Websites

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of these websites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of these third parties before providing any personal information.

7. Your Rights

Under the Data Privacy Act of 2012, you have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • The right to be informed that your personal information will be, are being, or were, collected and processed.
  • The right to object to the processing of your personal information.
  • The right to reasonable access to your personal information.
  • The right to rectify or correct your personal information if it is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • The right to order the destruction or blocking of your personal information.
  • The right to damages sustained due to inaccurate, incomplete, outdated, false, unlawfully obtained, or unauthorized use of your personal information.
  • The right to data portability.
  • The right to lodge a complaint with the National Privacy Commission (NPC).

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the contact information provided below.

8. Changes to this Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal obligations. We will post any changes on this page and update the "Last Updated" date. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically.
